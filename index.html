The Motivation

What is Funny Brains?

Funny Brains is an initiative that integrates humour and creative communication into neuroscience outreach. It provides a platform for researchers to present scientific concepts through comedy and storytelling, enhancing accessibility, audience engagement and the broader impact of their research.

The Method

Like good comedy, everything follows the rule of three: three mentors, three parts of the workshop, and three versions of Funny Brains — two in-person and one online. The mentors bring varied experience in neuroscience and science communication.

3 Mentors  ·  3 Workshop Parts  ·  3 Editions Because the rule of three works in comedy — and in science communication.
The Workshop Structure
01

The Tutorial

Participants kick off with a Science Communication Talk, an Ice-Breaker, and a Brainstorm. In small groups guided by mentors, they develop their core science comedy concept.

  • Science Communication Talk
  • Ice-Breaker activities
  • Small-group brainstorm with mentor guidance

The goal is not about being a comedian — it's about storytelling, being relatable and human.

02

The Feedback

Before the performance, a subset of participants meet up with the mentors to hone their materials and shake off any pre-show stage fright.

Performers practise their routine in front of each other, and mentors give targeted feedback on flow and delivery.

03

The Performance

Performers take the stage before crowds of a few hundred conference attendees. Acts range from quick-fire jokes to classic stand-up about conference life to sketch comedy about neurons.

The Benefits

For Participants

  • Improved science communication skills
  • Public speaking and performance experience
  • Cross-disciplinary networking
  • New perspectives on their own research
  • Authentic, human communication style

For Audiences

  • Accessible and engaging neuroscience content
  • Increased interest and connection with different areas of scientific research
